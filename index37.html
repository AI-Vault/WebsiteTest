Google Is Using On-Device AI to Spot Scam Texts and Investment Fraud
Google is enhancing its on-device AI capabilities in the Google Messages app to combat digital fraud, particularly scams originating from text messages. With Americans losing over $16.6 billion to online scams last year, including significant amounts due to text message scams, Google aims to empower users with advanced Scam Detection features. This AI detects various types of scams, including cryptocurrency, impersonation, and technical support scams, flagging approximately 2 billion suspicious messages monthly without sharing user data with Google. The challenges include identifying complex scams, like long-term investment frauds that build trust over time. Google’s technology may eventually extend to phone call protections. This initiative reflects a broader industry trend where companies are leveraging AI to combat scams and protect consumers.
