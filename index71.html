Microsoft Cuts Off Access to Bing Search Data as It Shifts Focus to Chatbots (wired:15-5-2025)

Microsoft announced it will stop providing access to its Bing Search APIs to boost its focus on AI chatbots, ending a crucial resource for smaller search engines and developers. Scheduled for August 11, this shift affects mainly smaller clients while larger users, including DuckDuckGo, will retain access. The company framed this transition as a response to market demands for AI solutions, though many developers have criticized the new AI-powered system as insufficient. This decision follows increased pricing for the APIs and layoffs at Microsoft, raising concerns among clients about reliability and dependencies on the tech giant. The move may influence the competitive search landscape, especially as Google faces antitrust scrutiny that could require it to share its own search data. Ultimately, the changes signal a significant pivot in how search is accessed and utilized in the evolving digital ecosystem. link

Other companies, like Meta and British telecom O2, are also deploying AI to counter scams, indicating a broader industry effort against digital fraud. Google is testing further expansion of scam detection into phone calls, showing its commitment to advancing security measures in communications. link

Google’s Advanced Protection for Vulnerable Users Comes to Android (wired:15-5-2025)

Google is introducing an Advanced Protection mode for Android 16, aimed at shielding at-risk users, such as activists and journalists, from rising threats like mercenary spyware. This mode imposes stringent security settings across apps and services to enhance data isolation and reduce exposure to unsecured communications. Key features include Intrusion Logging for monitoring potential compromises and the Memory Tagging Extension (MTE) for preventing attacks targeting memory vulnerabilities. Notably, some restrictions, like blocking connections to legacy 2G networks, can't be turned off. While most features will launch with Android 16, Intrusion Logging will arrive later, alongside enhanced USB protection. Google's initiative signifies a notable shift towards consumer-focused mobile security, leveraging on-device AI for responsive monitoring while balancing usability. link
