Building better cities

Clara Brenner and Julie Lein, who met at MIT's Sloan School, co-founded the Urban Innovation Fund (UIF), a VC firm focused on urban innovation. Starting with $24.5 million in 2016, UIF has invested in 64 startups aimed at addressing urban challenges such as transportation, public health, and education. The firm prioritizes businesses that combine social impact with potential high returns, often focusing on underrepresented founders. Brenner’s background in commercial real estate and Lein’s in political consulting complemented each other, allowing them to succeed in segments that traditional investors often overlook. Key investments include Electriphi, a software firm for EV charging, which was later acquired by Ford. The success of UIF has contributed to the growing acceptance of impact investing within mainstream venture capital. Brenner and Lein emphasize the importance of collaboration and trust in their partnership, which mirrors the values they seek in the startups they support. link

There’s AI Inside Windows Paint and Notepad Now. Here’s How to Use It

AI tools have been incorporated into the Windows Paint and Notepad applications, enhancing their functionalities for users. In Paint, a Copilot feature provides options like Image Creator for generating images based on text prompts, Generative Erase for removing objects, and Remove Background for isolating subjects. The Image Creator requires AI credits linked to a Microsoft account, whereas the other two features are free. In Notepad, the Copilot feature allows users to modify existing text, offering functionalities such as making selected text shorter or longer, changing its tone, and reformatting it into lists or poetry. A rewrite function previews various changes before application. Both applications allow users to disable the Copilot button if desired. The integration of AI emphasizes the growing trend of enhancing standard software tools with advanced capabilities. link
