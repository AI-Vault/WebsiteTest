How to split the bill without causing long-term divisions

The article addresses the complexities of splitting bills among friends, particularly in contexts like dining out or shared living expenses. It highlights the emotional strain and potential conflict stemming from financial disparities in friendships, noting that a significant percentage of people have felt financially incompatible with friends. Suggestions include fostering open discussions about costs to ensure clarity before expenses are incurred. Various bill-splitting apps, such as Splitwise, Splid, and Tricount, are recommended to facilitate fair distribution of costs without awkwardness. Additionally, some banks offer built-in bill-splitting tools that can aid in managing shared expenses more efficiently. The overall theme revolves around maintaining equitable financial practices in friendships to prevent misunderstandings and preserve relationships.

Building better cities

Clara Brenner and Julie Lein, who met at MIT's Sloan School, co-founded the Urban Innovation Fund (UIF), a VC firm focused on urban innovation. Starting with $24.5 million in 2016, UIF has invested in 64 startups aimed at addressing urban challenges such as transportation, public health, and education. The firm prioritizes businesses that combine social impact with potential high returns, often focusing on underrepresented founders. Brenner's background in commercial real estate and Lein's in political consulting complemented each other, allowing them to succeed in segments that traditional investors often overlook. Key investments include Electriphi, a software firm for EV charging, which was later acquired by Ford. The success of UIF has contributed to the growing acceptance of impact investing within mainstream venture capital. Brenner and Lein emphasize the importance of collaboration and trust in their partnership, which mirrors the values they seek in the startups they support.

What to do if your phone is lost or stolen: practical steps to restore peace of mind

The article provides a comprehensive guide on steps to take if a smartphone is lost or stolen. It emphasizes the importance of acting quickly to protect personal data and prevent potential fraud. The recommended immediate actions include using \"Find My\" features to locate or lock the phone, contacting the network provider to block the SIM, reporting the incident to the police, and changing passwords for sensitive accounts, especially email. Additional advice is offered for when a new phone is acquired, such as setting strong security measures (PINs and biometric locks), activating theft protection features, and regularly backing up data. The article highlights the dual focus on both immediate recovery tactics and long-term preventative measures to enhance smartphone security and personal data protection. Key themes include personal security, data protection, and proactive measures against theft.

Driving business value by optimizing the cloud

Organizations are increasingly investing in cloud technologies to enhance business agility and innovation, with global spending on cloud infrastructure services reaching $84 billion in Q3 2024, reflecting a 23% year-on-year increase. However, many workloads remain onsite and not optimized, preventing potential growth. Cloud optimization is crucial for companies to balance cost, security, resilience, and innovation, allowing for efficient resource allocation to reduce expenses while maximizing performance. Benefits include enhanced security, resilient workloads, customer experience protection, increased revenue, and savings that can be reinvested into innovation projects like generative AI. The article emphasizes the importance of optimizing cloud spending beyond mere cost-cutting to fuel growth and innovation opportunities.
